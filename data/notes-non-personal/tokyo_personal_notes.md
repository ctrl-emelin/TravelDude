---
destination: Tokyo
title: My Tokyo Notes
---
The teamLab Borderless digital art museum is worth every yen — book online at least 2 weeks ahead. 
The Shibuya Sky rooftop observation deck is better than Tokyo Skytree for photos because you get the Skytree IN the frame.

For ramen: Fuunji in Shinjuku for tsukemen (dipping ramen), Ichiran anywhere for a solo late-night bowl. 
Depachika (department store basement food halls) are incredible for lunch sets — Isetan in Shinjuku is the best.

Getting around: IC card (Suica or Pasmo) is essential. JR Pass only worth it if you're doing Shinkansen trips. 
The local Tobu Skytree Line is cheaper than JR for Asakusa.

Hidden gem: Yanaka neighborhood for old Tokyo vibes, no tourists, great street food and cats.
Koenji for vintage shopping and live music — totally different vibe from the touristy east side.

Practical: 7-Eleven has the best convenience store food. Family Mart onigiri are also excellent.
Google Translate camera mode is your best friend for menus.
Pocket wifi from the airport rental counter — worth it even if your phone has international data.
